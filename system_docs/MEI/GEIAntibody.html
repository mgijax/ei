Introduction

(This information has been extracted from the GXD Schema document)

A critical aspect of describing any expression assay is to describe the probe used. In the case of protein-based assays, such as Western blots and Immunohistochemistry, the probes used are antibodies which recognize specific protein targets called antigens.

An antibody:

  • is raised against a specific antigen and is raised in a specific species.
  • may detect the antigen target in either or both Western blotting and Immunoprecipitation columns.
  • recognizes the products of one or more genes.
